🍒 Fruit picking — not available in May
Hardanger fruit picking season: strawberries/cherries from early July, apples/pears September–October. In May the trees are in blossom — spectacular to walk through, but nothing to pick yet. Good news: farm shops are open year-round selling cider, apple juice, jams and local cheese — you can buy and taste even if you can't pick. Roadside stalls appear later in summer.

Norway practical tips
🛒
Sunday shopping
Most supermarkets closed on Sundays. Only Bunnpris (Bergen, 24/7) stays open. Stock up on Saturdays.
⚡
EV charging
Norway has great EV infrastructure. Use Recharge or Plugsharing apps. Always charge overnight at each stay.
🌧
Bergen weather
Bergen is one of Europe's rainiest cities. Pack waterproofs for everyone. Always have a rainy-day plan.
🍷
Alcohol rules
Wine & spirits only from Vinmonopolet state shops. Supermarkets sell beer only. Vinmonopolet closed Sundays.
💳
Cash-free country
Norway is almost entirely card-based. You'll rarely need cash — even small cafés and markets take cards.
🕐
Early/late light
May 1–13: Bergen sunrise ~05:50, sunset ~21:30. Oslo sunset ~21:40 by May 13. Lots of daylight for activities.
